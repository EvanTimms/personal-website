SPEAR

Space Exploration And Robotics

Founded in 2017, SPEAR (SPace Exploration And Robotics) is a student group from the University of Alberta. The purpose of the group is the design and construciton of a Mars rover which is to be entered into the Mars Societies University Rover Challenge based in Utah. This competion allows different universities to compete over a set of challenges that are designed to simulate to real world challenges that current Mars Rovers have. The winner of the competition is a cash prize and the chance to present at the annual Mars Society Convenction.

My main role at SPEAR was the design of the software that operated the rovers robotic arm. This arm would have to operate with a full 6 degrees of freedom (forward,backward,up/down,left/right, yaw, pitch, roll) and be able to communicate with the rovers operating system. The rover uses a Nividia Jetson as its central computer and some smaller raseberry pi's that manage arm and sensor functionality.
